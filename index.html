Hi there!

I'm a graduate assistant at the Human-Centered Artificial Intelligence Research Laboratory at the University of Central Florida, a new sister lab to the Complex Adaptive Systems Laboratory.

I want to help turn AI into a force for social good in a field where scholars and engineers so often disregard the social consequences of the technologies they create. I believe that by marrying social science with computer science, we can both make better AI and garner a better understanding of our society, our technologies, and ourselves. With this comes the understanding of how we can transform our society into a more sustainable, equitable, and compassionate place for everyone.

My research interests to this end are broad and range from work in fundamental theoretics to immediate real-world applications:

  • I've helped robots understand human intent so that we can get along with them a little bit better.
  • I've analyzed the evolution of entire cultures over millennia to get a better grip on how these "society" things of ours grow and change in the long-run.
  • A project I helped lead is now in low Earth orbit (though that one was just for fun).
  • I helped create (and currently co-steward) the worlds most complete dataset of archaeological radiocarbon dates so that we can better understand what did and didn't work for our ancestors.
  • My master's thesis is about using AI to understand the social factors that help foster sustainable irrigation systems.
